Problem Statement

Concrete today is probably the most widely used construction material in the world. This is due not only to wide range of applications that concrete offers, but also its great strength, affordability, durability and versatility. It consist of rationally chosen mixture of binding material such as lime or cement, well graded fine and coarse aggregates, water and admixtures(to produce concrete with special properties). In a concrete mix, cement and water form a paste or matrix which in addition to filling the voids of the fine aggregate, coats the surface of fine and coarse aggregates and bind them together.

Aggregates occupy 70-80 per cent of the volume and have considerable influence on the properties of the concrete. Aggregate are the materials basically used as filler in concrete. The coarse aggregate form the main matrix of concrete and the fine aggregate form the filler matrix between the coarse aggregate. The properties to be considered while selecting aggregate for concrete are strength, particle, shape, specific gravity, bulk density, voids, porosity, moisture content, and bulking.

Why to replace conventional aggregate?

With day by day increasing constructions in society, the demand of aggregates is rising exponentially, leading to the continuous running of the processes like quarrying, blasting, mining etc. of the mountains. It is also raising many environment and nature earth concerns. So it’s not impossible to think that in upcoming future there would be a crisis of aggregate. We engineers should feel a moral obligation to tackle such challenges and start searching for an economic, feasible, long run and environment friendly substitute for the conventional aggregate.

Objective : - To minimise the amount of conventional aggregate or fully eliminate conventional aggregate used to make concrete and find the best suitable substitute for its replacement in present conditions.

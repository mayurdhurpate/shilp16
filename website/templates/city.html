    • "Choosing to save a river is more often an act of passion than of careful calculation. You make the choice because the river has touched your life in an intimate and irreversible way, because you are unwilling to accept its loss." — (David Bolling).

    Conclave is an event which will be held for the very first time in SHILP. In this event, we will organize a meet which will be presided by a panel of 6 members, each of which will be an expert in their respective fields, relating to the topic of discussion. The topic of the discussion in the conclave will be “River Water Rejuvenation”.

    The government has spent a staggering Rs.20, 000 crores on repeated attempts for the rejuvenation of the rivers. But the efforts have borne no results. The question that stares us all in the face is “Where have we gone wrong?” with the aim to answer this probing question, the Department of Civil Engineering, IIT BHU invites participation for the conclave.

    At SHILP, we believe that there are students, with many innovative ideas. All they need is a platform, Conclave will be their platform to promote those ideas to panel members, so that they can actually be put to use. So,in the first round, candidates will submit a paper to present their ideas. Out of those candidates, some will be selected on the basis of quality and effectiveness of their solutions. They will be invited to SHILP ‘16 to discuss their ideas in the presence of the panel.

    Based on the overall performance, the best candidates will be awarded.


    Important
    1) The first round of the event will be conducted online, with the solutions to the given problem statement to be submitted by mail.
    2) The last date for Registration and Abstract submission is on 15 September
    3) Based on the criterion of judgement, the best 10 solutions will be selected and will be invited to the second round of the event at IIT BHU Varanasi, to be held during summer
    4) Here, the participants have to elaborate their submissions in front of a panel of judges, and defend their solutions in terms of feasibility, practicability, cost of operation, etc.
    5) Based on their overall performance, the top three submissions will be rewarded.
    6) Certificates of participation and merit will be awarded to the participants of first round and second round respectively. Also, loads of goodies to be won ;) .

    Guidelines for Abstract

    An abstract is a brief summarizing statement, usually between 150 and 300 words long. It gives the reader a synopsis of the problem, method, results and conclusions of your document. Abstract should be descriptive enough to give judicial committee of your work.
    A. This should be a pdf document containing these major elements:
    1. Problem: Define the main problem you are concerned with.
    2. Method: State the main approach you used to solve the problem. Describe it in brief.
    3. Feasibility: Give examples of how and where you expect to implement the techniques you described.
    B. Formatting Guidelines
    1. Document should start with the problem you are working on,in Times New Roman with the font size “14” which should be bold and centrally aligned.
    2. Author Name(s) should be written immediately after Titlein Times New Roman with the font size “12” and this should also be centrally aligned.
    3. The main content should be in Times New Roman with the font size “11”. Use of Bold, Italic, and Underline in the main content is strictly prohibited.
    C. Sending Abstract
    1. All the abstracts should be sent to shilp@iitbhu.ac.in.
    2. The subject of the mail should be Conclave Abstract.
    3. Abstract should be attached as a pdf file with its name in following format: title_Name of first author.pdf
    D. Plagiarism will lead to direct disqualification.
    E. Maximum number of participants for a paper is 4 and one student can be a part of more than 1 paper.

    The Ganga River is one of the largest rivers in the world. Ganga is considered as the holiest river in India with massive spiritual, religious and ritual significance. As a result of the industrial revolution, the Ganga River has worked its way up to being one of the world’s most polluted rivers, with the level of pollutants being more than 3000 times than the permissible limit defined by the WHO as ‘safe’. Despite of the various schemes and action plans in place currently for cleaning up the river, the pollution levels of the river are not reducing.
    1) Industrial wastes/STP - Each day, more than 500 million liters of wastewater from industrial sources are dumped directly into Ganga. In many places, this wastewater entering the rivers is completely raw, completely untreated. Without adequate and affordable treatment technologies, small-scale industries continue to pollute the River Ganga with over a billion litres of toxic chemicals. Most STPs built under the Ganga Action Plan don’t effectively treat the heavy metal toxins and thus the even treated waters released into the river remain toxic.
    2) Religous ceremonies by riverside - Dead Bodies, food, waste, leaves etc find their way in the Ganga for ritualistic reasons. Mass bathing during festive seasons and unburned human corpses thrown into the river contribute the most towards the degradation of Ganga.
    3) Dams and pumping stations - Adding more and more hydroelectricity projects on Ganga "Aviral Dhara" of the rier is hampered ie adequate water is not available from the barrage to dilute the polluted water reaching the main river during the dry seasons of year.The problem requires a middle path between "Electricity for All" and "Restoring River's Flow"
    4) Domestic Wastes: rural India - 50 major cities housing about 500 million people lying along the Ganga's main stem, contribute about 2,200 MLD (Millions litres a day) of domestic waste. Moreover, about 1,650 gram panchayats lie directly on the banks of the Ganga. The domestic waste and sewage they generate is almost entirely untreated. There is a dire need for a all new waste treatment method for Rural India.
    5) Agricultural and Solid Waste - Non-point sources of pollution from agricultural run-off containing residues of harmful pesticides and fertilisers and Solid garbage thrown directly into the river are also major contributors.

TORQUE (PRIZE WORTH RS.6000)

Introduction

Task

Judging Criteria
INTRODUCTION

Hydro-electricity is one of the most prominent sustainable form of electricity as hydroelectricity is generated using the kinematic energy from a turbine which receives the force from moving water. The above description makes it quite easy to understand that the turbine plays a very crucial role in generation of hydro-electricity.

Modern turbines are very efficient but there is always room for improvement so Shilp’16 hosts torque to give a stage to all of you with those innovative ideas stacked up in your heads. In this event participants are supposed to design and build a hydro turbine to convert the mechanical energy from a falling stream of water to electrical energy. So participate in this event and learn and build something, not just for winning but for protecting the environment.

1.0 Aim:

Teams are supposed to design and build a hydro turbine for a falling stream of water, and convert the harnessed mechanical energy to electrical energy. The hydro turbine will be attached to a DC dynamo and the teams will be judged on the basis of their electrical output. The judges will have access to two terminal coming out of the dynamo.

1.1 Arena:

The arena consists of a water discharge valve (tap) which will discharge water at a constant head and at constant flow rate. The discharge valve will be present at a height of 1.5m from ground.

1.2 Rules:

1. Teams can use any material to build turbine.
2. There is no restriction on size, type and orientation of axis of turbine.
3. Only DC output will be analysed for judging.
4. Teams are free to bring their own dynamo.
5. Load variable resistance (up to 10 kΩ) will be provided by Team Shilp to get output (refer: Maximum Power Point Theorem).
6. Teams are free to connect pipes or extensions to modify water flow from water discharge valve.

1.3 Judging:

Teams will be judged on the basis of their DC electrical output only. More the electrical power generated by the design, more points will be awarded. A load resistance(R) (rheostat up to 10 kΩ) will be provided for the measurement of DC output. Teams will be judged on the basis of their DC electrical output only. More the electrical power generated by the design, more points will be awarded. A load resistance(R) (rheostat up to 10 kΩ) will be provided for the measurement of DC output.
The correct value of load resistance can be determined by trial and error method (refer: MPPT). Judges will use a digital ammeter (least count: 1µA) and digital voltmeter (least count: 1mV) to determine the electrical power output.

    INTRODUCTION

    Carbon dioxide (CO2) is the primary greenhouse gas emitted through human activities. In 2014, CO2 accounted for about 80.9% of the world greenhouse gas emissions from human activities. Carbon dioxide is naturally present in the atmosphere as part of the Earth's carbon cycle (the natural circulation of carbon among the atmosphere, oceans, soil, plants, and animals). Human activities are altering the carbon cycle–both by adding more CO2 to the atmosphere and by influencing the ability of natural sinks, like forests, to remove CO2 from the atmosphere. While CO2 emissions come from a variety of natural sources, human-related emissions are responsible for the increase that has occurred in the atmosphere since the industrial revolution.

    SOURCE OF CO2 EMISSIONS

    There are both natural and human sources of carbon dioxide emissions. Natural sources include decomposition, ocean release and respiration. Human sources come from activities like cement production, deforestation as well as the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas.

    EXTENT OF CO2

    Human CO2 emissions continue to grow at a business-as-usual pace with a record set in 2010 for the largest emissions ever

    EFFECTS OF CARBON DIOXIDE (CO2) EMISSION

    1) The extra carbon dioxide increases the green house effect. More heat is trapped by the atmosphere, causing the planet to become warmer than it would be naturally. The increase in global temperature this causes is called global warming.
    2) CO2 in the atmosphere is having negative effects on plants and the quality of wheat crops.
    3) As the concentration CO2 in air rises it can cause headaches, dizziness, confusion and loss of consciousness.
    4) Damage to marine ecosystems as CO2 rises at higher concentrations.

A growing concern is that the large ice sheets of Greenland and West Antarctica will likely melt more quickly in the future, accelerating the rise in sea levels and threatening many coastal communities. The Dutch, much of whose land is already below sea level, are so concerned they have begun to experiment with floating houses. Extreme Weather Many scientists believe that the increase in heat waves, episodes of extreme rainfall, and the intensity of hurricanes may be related to climate changes caused by global warming, and that we can expect harsher weather if the warming trend is allowed to continue. Droughts Rising temperatures may increase the number of droughts, which will in turn affect flood crops and water availability across the globe. Many scientists are warning that we may already be seeing agricultural problems as a result of global climate change. Economic Downturn In addition, recent economic studies warn of the economic consequences of climate change. One warns that they could be as bad, or worse, than the Great Depression of the 1930s.

    DTH (PRIZE WORTH RS.6000)

    Introduction

    Task

    Judging Criteria

    While many of you might have experienced the thrills of going on a memorable road trip to a hill station in one of those summers, did you ever wonder about the challenges faced by civil engineers in designing those curves, bends and gradients and the need to incorporate them in the road design??

    Here, Shilp’16 presents you Down The Hill, the first of its kind road building competition where participants are required to build a road model for a hilly region incorporating some specified design features. The event will test one’s ability to design a structure in restricted conditions, hence simulating the experience of hill road building.

    So, come along this fall and join us on an exciting road trip Down The Hill where your Construction meets your Creativity.

    Problem Statement

    Task
  • The participants are required to build a track of road model for a regular sized marble. The model is to be assembled on spot within a given time frame. The track model is to be tested on the basis of its design, its aesthetics and the time taken by the marble to roll down the track. Area provided: (1.75x1.75 m²)
  • NOTE: Students have to bring their own track and any other material required for making the track.
    • General Design Rules
  • 1. The road track should be designed inside the ply board area(1.75x1.75 m²).
  • 2. The road track model should be designed for a regular size, steel or glass marble.
  • 3. The road track should have a maximum gradient (slope) of 30⁰ throughout the path. Any road track model exceeding this upper limit in its entire or partial length will be disqualified.
  • 4. The road track should pass through two mandatory points:-
      i. A (the starting pt of the track with elevation 120cm),
      ii. B (elevation 60 cm) ;the location of points A and B are as shown in figure 1.
  • 5. The road track must include at least one looping turn and one hairpin turn anywhere in the track length.
  • 6. During its run from A to B, all the looping turns should be made about the vertical axis through point A as shown.
  • 7. During its run from B to the zero elevation point, the participants are free to include any feature to their tracks to gain more points.
  • 8. The marble to be rolled down the track will be provided by Team Shilp.
  • 9. The marble will be rolled down the track thrice to measure the minimum time taken in the run among the three trials.
  • 10. The marble should neither go off the track nor should it ever stop during its run. If this happens, that trial will be considered invalid.
  • 11. The motion of marble on the track must only be under the effect of gravity.
  • 12. Failure to complete the run even once in the three trials will lead to disqualification of the participant(s).
    •

    THE ARENA

    Time(25_ Points)
  • The marble is to be rolled down the track thrice and the time of run will be noted for each run.
  • The minimum of these three times will be taken as the official time.
    • The points will be relative and will be calculated as below Points=(Min Time/Your Time)*25

    Design(50_ Points)

  • 1. Looping turns(15_ Points)
  • A turn in the track made such that the curved section crosses over itself so as to form loop in top view of the track will be referred to as looping turn. Looping turn are the most important feature in construction of hill road. They help in reaching from one point to another maintaining a slope that is safe and practical in most cases. Points will be relative and will be calculated as below Points = (Sum of diameters of your looping turns/Maximum sum of diameters)X15.
  • 2. Hairpin turns(10_ Points)
    • A turn with a very acute angle such that the marble turns almost 180oin its run will be termed as hairpin turn. At sharp horizontal curves it becomes necessary to provide hair-pin bends. Because of precipitous rock, deep valley , steep ascends to obligatory points and presence of innumerable gorges, hair pin bends are unavoidable on hill roads. The hairpin turns should be strictly U-shaped. Points will be relative and will be calculated as below Points = (No. of hairpin turns in your track/Maximum number of hairpin turns from among all the tracks)X10
  • 3. Horizontal track length(15_ Points)
    • The part of the track being horizontal and having minimum length of 20 cm will be considered for the horizontal track length.
    Points will be relative and will be calculated as below Points = (Total length of your Horizontal track/Maximum total length of the horizontal track among all the tracks) X 15 *Models having continuous horizontal track length less than min specified(25 cm) will be awarded zero points.
  • 4. Smooth run(10_ Points)
    • Comfortable journey along the road is of prime concern in any kind of terrain. Run of the marble should be smooth with minimum collisions and undulations at the corners and while leaving the track. A maximum of 10 points will be awarded based on the judges' discretion.
  • 5. Aesthetics(25_ Points)
  • The tracks should be creative and sophisticated in design. Incorporating other design features of the road to make it more realistic will fetch extra points for the participants. The structure and elegance of the design will be judged to decide points on the basis of the aesthetics.

    • RULES AND REGULATIONS:

    • 1. Event is open to all.
    • 2. Maximum team size is 4.
    • 3. Participants can form teams from different branches/college/university/institute.
    • 4. No two teams must have any common member.
    • 5. Decision of the judges shall be final. Any model violating the rules above or spirit of the competition will be disqualified.

    IMPACTO (PRIZE WORTH RS.6000)

    Introduction

    Task

    Judging Criteria
    INTRODUCTION

    Essentially, shock absorbers do two things. Apart from controlling the movement of springs and suspension, shock absorbers also keep your tyres in contact with the ground at all times. At rest or in motion, the bottom surface of your tyres is the only part of your vehicle in contact with the road. Moreover used for launching rovers on planet with no harm.

    So we provide you a platform to bring out the potential Civil Engineer inside you and to show your designing skills here you have to design a shock absorbing mechanism with given specifications.

    Task

    The participants are required to design a shock absorbing mechanism which can sustain maximum impulse when payload dropped from a given height.



    Specifications

    Design Constraints

  • • Length, breadth and height (individually) of the model should be in range 30 to 50 cm.
  • • A wooden board should be attached horizontally above the mechanism to provide space for testing.
  • • Wooden board should be of thickness 5mm.
  • • Payload will be provided by team Shilp.


    • Game play

    Testing will be done by following set of procedure:

  • • Teams will be given 5 minutes to make final changes in their structure before the testing, and once the changes are done, the structure will be weighed. After weighing is done no changes can be made in the structure.
  • • The dimensions of the structure will be measured and it will be made sure that the horizontal part of the structure is perfectly horizontal with the help of a bubble spirit.
  • • Model will be analysed for cost evaluation.
  • • Payload will be dropped on mechanism from 3 different heights ( 0.5 , 1.0 , 1.5 m ) and impulse will be recorded for each trial ( a, b, c respectively )
  • • Weight of the payload is 500 gm.


    • Definition of failure

    The structure is considered failed when any of the following happens:

  • • If dimension of model is not according to provided data.
  • • Breaking of model will be considered as failure.
  • • If the board is not horizontal enough for testing.


    • Rules
  • • Once the structure is weighed, you are not allowed to modify your structure in any way.
  • • If any of these constraints are not met, point deductions (as mentioned) or disqualification may be imposed at the sole discretion of the organizers.
  • • Any team that is not ready at the time specified will be disqualified immediately.
  • • Judges' decision shall be final and binding on all.
  • • The organizers reserve all rights to change any or all of the above rules as they deem fit.


    • On the basis of following criteria marks will be given -
  • Cost Evaluation - Points will be awarded on the basis of cost of mechanism . 100 points will be given to minimum cost among all models and 0 to maximum and rest will be given according to linear interpolation.
  • Weight of Model - Points will be awarded on the basis of weight of model . 100 points will be given to minimum weight among all models and 0 to maximum and rest will be given according to linear interpolation.
  • Impulse-impulse will be recorded as - A = ( 0.2*a + 0.3*b + 0.5*c )
  • 100 points will be given to model having minimum value of A among all models and 0 to maximum and rest will be given according to linear interpolation.
  • Innovative points.-Extra points will be awarded on the basis of innovation .
  • 10 points will be deducted if the board is not horizontal.
  • In case of any changes in the rules or judging criteria the final decision lies with us.
